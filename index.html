About Me

Career Objective

To advance mechanical and aerospace engineering through research in aeroelasticity, vibration analysis, structural dynamics, finite element methods, composite structures, and control systems. I aim to integrate computational modeling, simulation techniques, and data-driven approaches, including machine learning and artificial intelligence, to develop innovative engineering solutions for complex real-world challenges.

My Academic Profile

I am an ambitious PhD Scholar at the Indian Institute of Technology (ISM) Dhanbad. Over seven years in academia as an Assistant Professor and lecturer, I have cultivated a deep expertise in mechanical design, computational methods, and machine design.

My current PhD research at IIT (ISM) Dhanbad involves combining physical structural mechanics with modern, data-driven machine learning models. I aim to optimize structural resilience, explore composite laminates, and map aeroelastic dynamic stability using powerful computing tools.

Research Focus Areas

Aeroelasticity

Exploring complex intersections of inertial, aerodynamic, and structural elastic forces. Investigating aerodynamic flutter thresholds and active stabilizing mechanisms.

Finite Element Analysis

Writing and utilizing solid finite element solvers to run stress formulations, modal analyses, and structural static/transient equations.

Structural Dynamics

Formulating structural dynamic responses, vibration attenuation parameters, transient behavior, and natural frequency harmonics.

Composite Structures

Analyzing layered, anisotropic structural components and laminate interfaces to identify performance under dynamic high-stress conditions.

Machine Learning (ML)

Implementing neural surrogate architectures, optimization protocols, and regression analytics to perform diagnostics and predictive structural analysis.

MATLAB & Simulink

Engineering control systems modeling, feedback mechanics, transient wave computations, and programmatic control algorithms.
