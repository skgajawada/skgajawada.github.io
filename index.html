About Me

Bridging classical mechanical foundations with modern data-driven computational methods.

Biography

Highly motivated mechanical engineer and former Assistant Professor with over 7 years of combined academic teaching and analytical research experience. Currently advancing scientific inquiries as a PhD Scholar at IIT (ISM) Dhanbad, focusing on structural engineering, modern computational dynamics, and aerospace design interfaces.

Specializes in coupling mechanical principles with predictive analytics, leveraging frameworks from Python, MATLAB/Simulink, and Dataiku to design, simulate, and analyze high-integrity mechanical systems.

Research Vision & Mission

Vision: To advance mechanical and aerospace engineering systems using aeroelasticity paradigms, finite element methods, structural health monitoring models, and active control systems.

Approach: Seamlessly integrating finite element modeling (FEM) with emerging architectures in Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence to synthesize lightweight, structurally robust, and smart composite structures capable of withstanding critical aerodynamic regimes.

Research Areas

Primary investigative thrusts shaping structural mechanics and computational research.

Aeroelasticity

Investigating critical aero-structural phenomena, focusing on flutter speed predictions, geometric non-linear boundary configurations, and stable structural architectures in high-speed flows.

Structural Dynamics

Modeling complex vibratory systems, dynamic modal shapes, system responses, and implementing active vibration reduction algorithms using MATLAB control loops.

Composite Structures

Analyzing lightweight multi-layered structures, orthotropic materials, layered laminate failures, and optimizing structural load transfer characteristics under static and fatigue patterns.

Finite Element Analysis

Formulating structural stiffness matrix mappings, discretization of complex configurations, static structural stress gradients, and thermal conduction grids.
