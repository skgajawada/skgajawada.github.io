I am a driven PhD Scholar within the Department of Mechanical Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology (ISM) Dhanbad. My core objective centers on advancing mechanical and aerospace domains by blending structural dynamics, active controls, and advanced computational mechanics with modern data-driven methodologies.

My path incorporates cross-disciplinary pillars, holding an AICTE QIP Postgraduate Certification in Data Science from IIT Bhilai alongside a foundational Master of Technology in Machine Design. This balance allows me to address complex physical mechanisms, such as aeroelastic flutter, by engineering advanced structural frameworks combined with modern artificial intelligence architectures.

"True engineering breakthroughs arise where rigorous classical mechanics converges gracefully with data-driven predictive architectures."

My architectural philosophy assumes that structural simulations shouldn't simply look backward at historical data, but must actively look forward via intelligent control mechanics. I treat programming code and mathematics as two halves of a single structural vocabulary designed to unlock durable, high-integrity modern aerospace frameworks.

My immediate research targets the engineering of digital twins for high-speed dynamic assemblies, mapping operational anomalies inside composite media using deep learning layers, and standardizing edge telemetry networks via federated optimization frameworks.

Research Projects

Dynamic Analysis & Active Vibration Control

Objective: Stabilizing thin-walled multivariable structural geometries subjected to dynamic structural shock loading matrices.

Methodology: Formulated state-space mathematical matrix algorithms coupled with Linear Quadratic Regulator (LQR) closed-loop control mechanics inside ANSYS, MATLAB, and Simulink execution environments.

FEM LQR Control MATLAB Simulink

Federated Learning for Intelligent Transportation Systems

Objective: Engineering distributed traffic orchestration frameworks without centralizing private edge client localized data.

Methodology: Designed decentralized parameter aggregation loops utilizing TensorFlow, Flower pipelines, and Python routines to enforce robust real-time localized prediction metrics.

Python TensorFlow Flower Edge AI

Hydraulic Robotic Arm Development

Objective: Building multi-axis pick-and-place manipulators operating under heavy torque profiles.

Methodology: Engineered system parameters based cleanly upon Pascal's Law to maintain continuous fluid-driven pressure transfer across varying mechanical loading axes.

Fluid Mechanics Pascal Law Robotics
